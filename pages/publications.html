Doctoral Thesis

Ph.D. Thesis 2025 UC Santa Cruz

The Flavor Program: Spanning Scales and Rare New Physics

Sri Aditya Gadam

This dissertation presents novel approaches to probing physics beyond the Standard Model through precision flavor physics. It comprises four major studies:

  • A supersymmetric extension of the Standard Model that explains the muon g-2 anomaly with multi-TeV sleptons
  • A supersymmetric flavor clockwork model addressing both the flavor puzzle and making predictions for flavor violation in the supersymmetric sector
  • An investigation of bottom-strange production at future muon colliders as a probe of the same new physics that might explain current B-decay anomalies
  • A study of rare Œõb decays at future Z-factories, introducing new observables that exploit baryon polarization to provide unique sensitivity to new physics

Together, these works demonstrate the extraordinary power of precision measurements to probe physics at energy scales far beyond the direct reach of current colliders.

Research Article 2021 arXiv:2106.09869

A Supersymmetric Flavor Clockwork

Wolfgang Altmannshofer, Sri Aditya Gadam

Clockwork models can explain the flavor hierarchies in the Standard Model quark and lepton spectrum. We construct supersymmetric versions of such flavor clockwork models. The zero modes of the clockwork are identified with the fermions and sfermions of the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model.
