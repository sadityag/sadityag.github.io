About Me

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Theoretical Physics at UC Santa Cruz, where I explored the mathematical frameworks that govern our universe at its most fundamental level. My research focused on understanding quantum mechanics, cosmology, and fundamental particle interactions.

Throughout my doctoral studies, I was particularly drawn to the elegant mathematical structures that emerge in theoretical physics and their potential to unlock new understanding about reality. My work has contributed to advancing our knowledge of quantum field theory and its applications in cosmology.

Beyond research, I'm passionate about science communication and making complex physics concepts accessible to broader audiences. I believe that sharing the wonder of physics can inspire the next generation of scientists and help society make informed decisions about scientific issues.

When I'm not immersed in equations and research papers, I enjoy exploring new ideas at the intersection of physics and philosophy, reading science fiction, and discussing the implications of modern physics with fellow researchers.
